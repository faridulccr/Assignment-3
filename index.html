Justice

Justice is a complex and multifaceted concept that has been the subject of much debate and discussion throughout history. At its most basic level, justice is the principle of fairness and equality in the distribution of rights and responsibilities.
However, there are many different interpretations and understandings of what constitutes justice, and different societies and cultures have developed their own systems and beliefs about what is just and unjust. One of the most important principles of justice is the idea of equality before the law. This principle holds that all individuals should be treated the same under the law, regardless of their race, gender, socioeconomic status, or any other characteristic.
This is a fundamental principle of many democratic societies, and is seen as essential for ensuring that everyone has an equal opportunity to access justice and have their rights protected. Another important principle of justice is the idea of proportionality. This principle holds that the punishment should fit the crime, and that the severity of the punishment should be proportional to the severity of the crime. This is seen as important for ensuring that justice is fair and that people are not punished excessively for minor offenses.

Flexbox is used for one-dimensional layouts, where elements are laid out in a single row or column. It is best used for small-scale layouts, such as aligning items within a container. Grid, on the other hand, is used for two-dimensional layouts, where elements are laid out in rows and columns. It is best used for larger-scale layouts, such as entire page layouts. Grid provides properties for defining the number of rows and columns, as well as for positioning items within those rows and columns.
